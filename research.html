Research Project

This section presents several research projects conducted by the research group/center over the last five years. The listed studies include both completed and ongoing research, focusing on the development of intelligent systems, artificial intelligence, and digital technologies to support innovation in agriculture, environmental monitoring, and behavioral analysis.


On Going & On Progress Research Project

Below is a list of research projects that are currently ongoing or under development by the research group/center. These studies focus on developing intelligent technologies, artificial intelligence, and digital technology applications to support innovation in agriculture and precision farming.


01

Smart Palm: AI-Driven Remote Sensing System for Intelligent Management of Oil Palm Plantations

Research Detail

Description

Smart Palm integrates remote sensing technologies like satellite and drone imagery with artificial intelligence for precision palm oil farming. The system enables real-time tree population monitoring, optimal fertilization recommendations, pest detection, and yield prediction, boosting productivity by up to 20% while minimizing waste in smallholder plantations.

Intern Staff

Elizabeth, Jennifer, Rexy, Owen, James, Elroi

02

AI-Driven Recommendation and Decision Support System for Integrated Tungro Pest Management in Rice Cultivation

Research Detail

Description

This intelligent recommendation and decision-making system is designed for integrated Tungro pest control in rice crops, integrating green leafhopper population monitoring, disease risk analysis, and action recommendations such as synchronized planting and resistant varieties. AI technology supports timely decision-making to minimize losses and enhance sustainable yields.

Intern Staff

Elizabeth, Jennifer, Rexy, Owen, James, Elroi

03

AI-Based Intelligent System for Monitoring Citrus Plant Health and Predicting Productivity Using Digital Image Analysis

Research Detail

Description

This intelligent system monitors citrus plant health and predicts productivity via digital image analysis of leaves and fruits, employing machine learning like CNN and YOLO to detect early diseases, ripeness, and yield estimation. The non-destructive approach aids farmers in optimizing care and harvesting with high field accuracy

Intern Staff

Elizabeth, Jennifer, Rexy, Owen, James, Elroi

04

Smart Bioacoustic: Intelligent System for Animal Identification and Behavior Recognition Using Sound Analysis

Research Detail

Description

Smart Bioacoustic is an intelligent system that identifies animal species and analyzes their behavior through AI-based sound recognition, such as automatic detection of bird calls, elephant vocalizations, or pest insects. It leverages PAM devices like AudioMoth and machine learning algorithms for real-time ecosystem monitoring, conservation, and illegal activity detection.

Intern Staff

Elizabeth, Jennifer, Rexy, Owen, James, Elroi


Completed Research Project

Below is a list of research projects that have been completed by the research group/center. One project, "Intelligent System for Non-Destructive Assessment of Plant Photosynthetic Pigments Using Digital Image Analysis", has been successfully concluded, contributing to the development of precision farming technologies.


01

Intelligent System for Non-Destructive Assessment of Plant Photosynthetic Pigments Using Digital Image Analysis

Research Detail

Description

This intelligent system develops a method for estimating plant photosynthesis pigment content, such as chlorophyll, carotenoids, and anthocyanins, using non-destructive analysis of leaf digital images. AI technologies like CNN, KNN, or ANN are applied to handle variations in illumination and camera quality, thereby enhancing the accuracy of plant health prediction in the field.

Intern Staff

Elizabeth, Jennifer, Rexy, Owen, James, Elroi
